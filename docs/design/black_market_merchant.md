# Design: Black Market Merchant Mini-Game & Passive Tree

**Status:** Draft for Discussion  
**Author:** Grok (based on user direction)  
**Date:** 2026  
**Related Systems:** Settlement Turns Economy (Development Turns, Idlem, Nursery, Events), Settlement (all buildings), Combat (loot & drops), Quests, Partners (Dispatch), Alchemy, Consume (body parts), Skills (gathering materials)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This document proposes a complete redesign of the Black Market from a set of fixed-cost vendor trades into a proper **merchant mini-game** integrated with the new Settlement Turns system.

**Core Fantasy:**  
You bring a mixed bundle of goods to a shady, opportunistic merchant who operates on "unconventional" sourcing. The merchant evaluates the total *Value* of what you offer (not a simple 1:1 trade). Higher value deals are more profitable but take longer to process using Development Turns. Over time, you invest in a passive tree (powered by the scarce resource **Idlem**) that lets you shape what kind of goods the merchant returns — more runes this week, more eggs next week, a chance at rare Consume parts when you really need them.

The system serves three purposes:
- A deep, replayable sink for excess or unwanted resources.
- A source of varied, desirable loot that feels personalized.
- One of the primary ways players engage with the "just one more turn" Settlement loop.

**Two Main Pillars:**
1. **The Merchant Mini-Game Loop** — Offering resources → Value calculation → Processing over Development Turns → Collecting results.
2. **Idlem-Powered Passive Tree** — Long-term investment that modifies processing time, base value, and reward composition.

---

## 2. Goals & Non-Goals

### Goals
- Replace the current simplistic fixed trades with an engaging, decision-rich mini-game.
- Create a strong, healthy sink for a wide variety of resources (timber, stone, bars, runes, keys, gear, bones, etc.).
- Make loot feel more dynamic and player-directed through the passive tree.
- Integrate deeply with the Settlement Turns system (processing time in Development Turns, event interactions).
- Reward both active engagement (careful offering, timing deals with events) and long-term investment (Idlem tree).
- Provide meaningful variety: players can chase different reward profiles on different days/weeks.

### Non-Goals
- Making the Black Market the single best source of any one resource (it should feel opportunistic and variable, not optimal).
- Complex real-time haggling or persuasion mechanics (keep it within Discord's interaction model).
- Allowing players to directly control exact rewards (the tree provides bias and chance, not guarantees).

---

## 3. Current State (What Exists Today)

The current Black Market (Tier 1–5) offers:
- Fixed gold cost for Equipment Caches (with tier loot bonus).
- Fixed rune costs for Rune Caches.
- Fixed Void Key cost for Boss Key Caches.
- 1 Unidentified Blueprint → 1-3 random special settlement materials (Magma Core, Life Root, Spirit Shard, etc.).
- Bulk trade options for the above.
- Standard building upgrade path.

**Problems:**
- Very transactional and repetitive.
- Almost no player agency in what they receive.
- No meaningful connection to the new turn-based development layer.
- Limited sink depth once the player has plenty of gold/runes/keys.

## 4. Proposed System Overview

### The Two Pillars

**Pillar 1: The Merchant Mini-Game**
- Player selects and offers a bundle of resources they currently hold.
- The system calculates a single **Value** score based on quantity, rarity, and current settlement state.
- Higher Value = better expected rewards + longer processing time (in Development Turns).
- The deal is sent to the merchant and processes over the specified number of turns.
- When complete, the player returns to collect a randomized reward package influenced by the Value and any active passive tree nodes.

**Pillar 2: Idlem Passive Tree**
- Unlocked and upgraded primarily using **Idlem** (generated by the Idlem Foundry via Development Turns).
- Nodes fall into three categories:
  - **Efficiency**: Reduce processing turns required.
  - **Value**: Increase the effective Value of offers.
  - **Bias / Targeting**: Allow the player to tilt reward composition toward specific categories (runes, skilling materials, eggs, Consume parts, gear, curios, gold, etc.).
- Higher investment unlocks stronger and more specific biases, including rare "jackpot" style outcomes.

## 5. The Merchant Mini-Game Loop (Detailed)

### 5.1 Offering Resources

The player can offer any combination of the following broad categories (exact list to be finalized):

- Settlement basics: Timber, Stone
- Processed materials: All bars, planks, essences
- Combat loot: Refinement/Potential/Shatter runes, boss keys, elemental keys
- Gear: Any equipment (weapons, armor, accessories, gloves/boots/helmets) — possibly with some value based on ilvl/rarity/passives
- Monster materials: All bone types
- Other: Curios, unidentified blueprints, etc.

The interface should support selecting multiple resource types and quantities in one offer (similar to how bulk trades currently work, but more flexible).

### 5.2 Value Calculation (Core of the Mini-Game)

This is the most important (and most tunable) part of the system. The goal is an **opaque but intuitive** system — players should develop a feel for what is "good" without needing a perfect spreadsheet.

High-level factors that should influence Value:
- Raw quantity of resources offered.
- Rarity/processing stage of materials (bars > raw ore, higher tier bones > low tier, etc.).
- Current market "demand" or "saturation" (could be lightly simulated or purely random with some memory).
- Settlement Tier (higher tier Black Market = better baseline Value).
- Player investment in certain passive nodes.

**Important Design Note:** The exact formula should *not* be fully transparent in-game. Players should experiment and develop heuristics ("Bringing a lot of high-tier bars seems to perform well when I'm chasing runes").

### 5.3 Processing Time

- Base processing time is derived from the final Value (higher Value = longer time).
- Passive tree nodes can reduce this time (sometimes significantly).
- Some events may temporarily increase or decrease processing times across the board.

Players should feel a real trade-off: "Do I take this decent 12-turn deal now, or hold out for something bigger that might take 30+ turns?"

### 5.4 Reward Generation

When the deal completes, the player receives a package whose composition is influenced by:
- The Value of the original offer (higher Value = better overall quantity/quality).
- Current passive tree biases (the biggest source of player agency).
- Any active events.

Reward categories should be broad and desirable:
- Combat power (runes, keys, gear)
- Settlement power (special materials, Idlem, workers via some mechanism)
- Horizontal/flavor (curios, eggs, Consume parts, pet-related items)

Rare high-Value deals + strong passive investment should occasionally produce exciting "jackpot" results (multiple giga eggs, rare Consume parts, large batches of high-tier runes, etc.).

## 6. The Idlem Passive Tree

This tree is the long-term progression hook for the Black Market.

### 6.1 Acquisition
- Nodes are unlocked and upgraded primarily with **Idlem**.
- Idlem is deliberately slow to generate (1–2 per turn via the Idlem Foundry).
- This makes the tree feel like a meaningful, multi-week/month investment.

### 6.2 Tree Structure (Proposed)

**Tier 1 – Efficiency (Early, cheap)**
- Reduce processing time by X% on all deals.
- Small chance to instantly complete small deals.

**Tier 2 – Value (Mid)**
- Flat or percentage increase to Value of all offers.
- Bonus Value when offering certain resource types (encourages variety).

**Tier 3 – Targeted Biases (The meat of the system)**
These should be toggleable or selectable so the player can change their "request" over time.

Examples of bias nodes:
- **Rune Focus**: Increased chance/quantity of refinement, potential, and shatter runes.
- **Skilling Focus**: Higher chance of skilling materials (various logs, ores, bones).
- **Egg Focus**: Chance for regular or rare monster eggs in the shipment.
- **Consume Focus**: Chance to yield "Mysterious Flesh" or other Consume-eligible body parts.
- **Gear Focus**: Better equipment caches or higher chance of good passives.
- **Curio / Puzzle Focus**: Increased curios or puzzle box components.
- **Gold / Liquid Focus**: Direct gold returns (less exciting but always useful).

Higher tiers of these nodes can add:
- Stronger bias percentages.
- "Guaranteed minimum" effects.
- Rare jackpot versions (e.g. "Giga Egg" chance, high-tier unique gear, etc.).

### 6.3 Design Philosophy for the Tree
- The tree should feel **transformative** but not mandatory for basic use of the Black Market.
- Players should be able to respec or have multiple "loadouts" eventually (though respec cost should be meaningful).
- Bias nodes are the main way players express preference ("This week I'm pushing Consume, next week I'm going hard on runes for upgrades").

---

## 7. Integration with Settlement Turns & Events

- Processing time is paid in **Development Turns**.
- Events can interact with active Black Market deals:
  - "Merchant is in a good mood" → faster processing or better Value on current deals.
  - "Supply line issues" → increased processing time.
  - "Sudden demand" → bonus Value on deals completed in the next X turns.
- The Black Market can itself be the subject of events (special one-time high-value offers, temporary bias events, etc.).

---

## 8. Risks & Mitigations

- **Value calculation opacity**: Mitigate with good tooltips, examples, and a "recent deals" log showing rough outcomes.
- **Feeling like gambling**: The system should feel *mostly* fair with positive expected value, with variance coming from the biases and rare jackpots rather than pure randomness.
- **Idlem becoming too important**: Keep the tree powerful but not completely mandatory. Basic functionality of the merchant should remain viable without heavy investment.
- **UI complexity**: The offering interface and passive tree need to be very clear and well-scaffolded.

## 9. Open Questions & Gaps to Fill

1. **Value Formula** — What are the actual weights and categories? How much should different resources be worth relative to each other?
2. **Exact processing time curve** — How long should a "good" deal take vs a "great" deal? (e.g. 5–8 turns for average, 15–30+ for excellent?)
3. **Reward generation tables** — How do we actually roll the final loot based on Value + biases?
4. **Passive tree node costs** (in Idlem) and exact power levels.
5. **Respec / flexibility** — Can players change their biases easily, or is there meaningful friction?
6. **How does the current tier system (Black Market building tiers) interact with the new merchant?** Does higher tier give baseline advantages?
7. **Any direct combat or quest integration** beyond just feeding resources?
8. **Visual / flavor** — What does the merchant look like? Do we want a recurring NPC character here too?
